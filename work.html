Event Production And Activations

The Cult Of More

The Cult of More reflects on what it means to build a more sustainable present and future. Hosted at Eito Chamberi by the creative startup Intrinsc, the exhibition shifts focus from finished objects to process: the thinking, questioning, and decisions behind each work. It invites visitors to reconsider how material and digital consumption patterns shape our environment, bodies, habits, and perception.

My role spanned early brainstorming and production problem-solving, artist outreach, and theme alignment across the exhibition. I managed spatial design and the curatorial flow of the works, and built an interactive interface to add participation to the event, collaborating closely with installation artist Violeta Vizuete to bring it to life.

Estudio Capilla

At Estudio Capilla, I assisted in the curation of the exhibited works and helped shape the artistic direction of the space, which was transformed into a model country convenience store for photographer Nathaniel Ryan Thompson. The installation explored disappearing third spaces in the rural South, centering on memory, nostalgia, and what’s quietly lost over time.

I managed social media and outreach through email and press releases, coordinated communication with local newspapers and journalists, and worked closely with the artist to follow and respect his creative vision. The opening was staged as a lively retail experience: art was sold like store merchandise, with a vintage working vending machine dispensing limited-edition prints and postcards. The environment leaned into a kitsch, “lost-in-time” design language, from throwback posters to nostalgic typography, reinforcing the project’s sense of place and cultural memory.
